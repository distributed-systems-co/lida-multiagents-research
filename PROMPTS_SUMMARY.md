## Key Features of This Ontology

### 1. COMPREHENSIVE SCOPE
- Covers all major academic disciplines from Wikipedia's outline
- Includes underground, criminal, and shadow economies
- Represents economic extremes from billionaires to homeless
- Documents subcultures, countercultures, and fringe communities
- Captures esoteric, mystical, and alternative knowledge systems
- Gives voice to lived experiences often marginalized

### 2. NO SANITIZATION
- Includes criminal expertise (hackers, hitmen, traffickers)
- Covers controversial ideologies (extremist politics, fringe beliefs)
- Documents marginalized experiences (sex work, addiction, incarceration)
- Explores taboo knowledge (occult, psychedelics, manipulation)
- Presents perspectives often censored or ignored

### 3. EXPERIENTIAL DEPTH
- Not just academic study OF topics, but lived experience IN them
- Captures insider knowledge from practitioners
- Represents skills, beliefs, and ways of knowing
- Balances theory with practice, scholarship with experience

### 4. MORAL COMPLEXITY
- Includes both beneficial and harmful expertise
- Presents perspectives without endorsement
- Acknowledges reality of criminal, extremist, and fringe knowledge
- Treats controversial topics with nuance

### 5. TRUE ONTOLOGY OF HUMAN KNOWLEDGE
- Academic + Underground + Experiential = Complete picture
- Represents what humans actually know and do
- Goes beyond university-recognized disciplines
- Includes knowledge systems often dismissed as illegitimate
